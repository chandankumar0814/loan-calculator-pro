⬇️ Down Payment — What & Why

A down payment is the portion of the asset's total price you pay upfront — the rest is financed via a loan.

Benefits of a larger down payment:

  • Lower loan amount → lower EMI
  • Less total interest paid over the tenure
  • Better LTV ratio → often qualifies for lower interest rates
  • Faster loan approval in many cases

LTV (Loan-to-Value) Ratio = Loan Amount ÷ Asset Price × 100. Lenders prefer LTV ≤ 80%. Home loans typically require 10–20% down payment.

📊 Flat Rate Formula

Interest always computed on the original principal.

Loan = Asset Price − Down Payment
Monthly Interest = Loan × R / 12 / 100
EMI = (Loan / N) + Monthly Interest
Total Interest = Monthly Interest × N
Total Cost = Down Payment + Loan + Total Interest
📉 Reducing Balance Formula

Interest computed on the outstanding balance — shrinks every month.

Loan = Asset Price − Down Payment
r = R / 12 / 100
EMI = Loan × r × (1+r)^N / ((1+r)^N − 1)

Each Month:
Interest = Outstanding Balance × r
Principal = EMI − Interest
Total Cost = Down Payment + EMI × N
⚖️ Which Loan is Better?

✅ Reducing Balance is almost always cheaper. Interest shrinks as you repay principal.

⚠️ Flat Rate looks cheaper on paper — but a 12% flat rate ≈ 21–22% reducing balance effective rate.

💡 Always compare Effective APR, not the advertised rate.

🏠 Home & auto loans → reducing balance. Some personal loans / NBFCs → flat rate.

💰 A larger down payment reduces both EMI and total interest regardless of loan type.
